Recipe

History

In 1955, a woman of the name Dorcas Reilly worked as a supervisor in the home economics division at Campbell's Headquarters in Camden, New Jersey. There, she was asked to create a dish that would be featured in the Associated Press. The recipe was based on ingredients you had around the house and this would have to include the Campbell mushroom soup and green beans. She and her team, toyed with many ingredients and ended up on the Green Bean Casserole we know today. It was cheap, took little time to make, and helped with more women joining the work-force because there was no one else at home to create such complex meals. Dorcas originally called this dish the Green Bean Bake, and was surprised how much of a hit it was! This recipe is printed onto the Campbell's mushroom soup can and is made during Thanksgiving by millions of Americans.

Ingredients

  1. One Can Campbell's Cream of Mushroom Soup
  2. Half Cup Milk
  3. One Teaspon Soy Sauce
  4. Four Cups Cooked, Uncut, Green Beans
  5. One and a Third French's French Fried Onions
    ‎
    ‎

Directions

  1. Heat your oven to 350°F.
  2. Stir the soup, milk, soy sauce, beans, and 2/3 onions into a dish. You can then season however you'd like.
  3. Bake the dish for 25 minutes, or until hot. Stir the mixture and add in the last 2/3 cup of onions.
  4. Bake for another 5 minutes, or until the onions are a golden brown.
  5. Let sit for 10 minutes before eating, enjoy!
