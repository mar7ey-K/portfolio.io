Journalism

Journalism is a career in which you produce and distribute reports on events going on around in the world to inform our society. What a journalist does is that they collect or gather information, whether it be from an article, interview, pictures, or anything factual, to then produce this information into a formal product which is then distributed out into the public. This career is an important factor of our society because it allows you to inform the public, hold others accountable, uncover the truth, provide a platform for diverse voices, and create a more informed and engaged society.

I would like to be a journalist because I really take passion and enjoyment from writing, and I would love for my writing to make an impact in some way. Another reason is because of how important of a job a journalist has and I want to make an impact with my work.

Education Requirements

The education requirements to become a journalist, at the minimum, is to achieve a bachelor's degree in journalism, communications, or any other related field, like English. To get a bachelor's degree you need to have around 4 years spent in college.

Main Skills

Some skills that are required, or necessary, to become a journalist generally is just the degree, but a plus would be to be very detail-oriented and also be very impartial in your writing. You're writing to inform, not give an opinoin, so lay the facts down.
